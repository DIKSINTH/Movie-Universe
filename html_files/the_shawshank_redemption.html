The Shawshank Redemption is a 1994 American prison drama film written and directed by Frank Darabont.

CRITICS

While The Shawshank Redemption received critical acclaim upon its release particularly for its story, the performances of Robbins and Freeman, Newman's score, Darabont's direction and screenplay and Roger Deakins cinematography.

BOX OFFICE

the film was a box-office disappointment, earning only $16 million during its initial theatrical run. Many reasons were cited for its failure at the time, including competition from the films Pulp Fiction and Forrest Gump, the general unpopularity of prison films, and even the title, which was considered to be confusing for audiences.
